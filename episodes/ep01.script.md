# Episode 1 Script

## cold_open_montage

you’re watching a country come apart.

barricades in the streets.
militias with rifles.
crowds chanting like it’s a holiday and a funeral at the same time.

the spanish civil war has started.

but the real mystery isn’t who fired the first shot.

it’s this:

how does a country split so quickly
that it starts behaving like several countries overnight?

## thesis

to understand the spanish civil war, you have to drop the simplest explanation.

this isn’t just left versus right.

it’s center versus regions.
city versus countryside.
old institutions versus new ones.

and sometimes it’s people on the same side
who can’t stand each other.

because before the war begins, spain already contains multiple political worlds.

## map_move_regions

spain looks like one shape on a map.

but it doesn’t feel like one place.

madrid is the capital — politically and legally.

but barcelona speaks catalan and runs an industrial economy of factories and unions.

the basque country has a language unrelated to spanish and a long tradition of autonomy.

andalusia in the south is dominated by huge estates and seasonal labor.

different languages.
different economies.
different histories.

so when national politics starts shaking…

these places don’t just argue.

they move.

## vignette_catalonia

start in catalonia.

barcelona in the 1930s is one of europe’s most turbulent industrial cities.

factories, unions, radical politics.

anarchists, socialists, labor movements — all competing for influence in the streets.

in catalonia, the idea of revolution isn’t abstract.

it’s something people organize for.

and when a city already has strong unions and a strong regional identity…

orders from madrid don’t carry the same weight.

barcelona checks its own watch.

## vignette_basque

now move north to the basque country.

a completely different political culture.

deep catholic tradition.
strong regional identity.
and a language older than any kingdom in spain.

basque nationalism isn’t about revolution.

it’s about autonomy.

and here’s the twist that makes spanish politics hard to simplify:

many conservative basques end up supporting the republic.

not because they love the republic…

but because the republic promises regional autonomy.

in spain, even political alliances are regional.

## vignette_andalusia

then there’s andalusia.

this is where politics becomes land.

huge estates dominate the countryside.

millions of laborers own nothing and work seasonally — if they work at all.

every year brings the same question.

will there be wages?

or hunger?

in places like this, ideology isn’t abstract.

it’s about land, wages, and survival.

when politicians argue about reform or order…

people hear something simpler:

will our lives change?

## the_contradiction

this creates the central contradiction of the spanish republic.

the new government wants to modernize spain.

schools.
civil rights.
land reform.
a new relationship between church and state.

but modernization forces one explosive question.

who gets to define what spain is?

a strong central government can enforce one answer.

a weak one cannot.

## pressure_gauge

during the 1930s, the political center begins to wobble.

elections become more polarized.

street violence increases.

and politics starts moving out of parliament…

into neighborhoods.

local committees form.
parties build militias.
groups prepare to defend their vision of the country.

because once the authority of the state becomes uncertain…

people start preparing for life without it.

## coup_as_accelerant

then, in july 1936, the military revolts.

a group of generals attempt to overthrow the republic.

the coup fails to seize the entire country.

instead, it splits spain in half.

and in many cities, the government arms unions and militias to resist the uprising.

power moves into the streets.

## mini_case_study_barcelona

barcelona becomes a preview of the new reality.

workers’ militias patrol neighborhoods.

committees run factories.

armed groups control checkpoints.

the official government still exists…

but it’s no longer the only authority.

parallel power has appeared.

## door_slam_multiple_authorities

this is the moment the spanish state fractures.

not into chaos.

into competition.

multiple groups claiming legitimacy.

multiple governments claiming authority.

politics is no longer just debate.

it’s something you can be stopped for in the street.

spain is still one country on a map.

but on the ground…

it has become many.

## so_what

the spanish civil war doesn’t simplify the country.

it exposes it.

different regions.
different economies.
different fears.

when the fighting begins, those layers don’t disappear.

they determine who fights.

who hesitates.

and who fractures.

## hook_ep02_land

and we haven’t even added the most explosive ingredient yet.

land.

because once you combine a weak state
with millions of people living on land they don’t own…

the map of spain stops being geography.

it becomes a fuse.

next episode:
the land question.
