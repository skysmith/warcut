## Current Thesis

The current episode framing is "many Spains": the war emerges from overlapping regional identities, labor conditions, autonomy struggles, class conflict, church/state conflict, and a weakening political center.

Operational rule: avoid flattening the conflict into a simple left-versus-right story. Keep local authority, region, labor, religion, and coalition-building visible.

## Research Backlog

Near-term episode support:

- July 1936 street conflict in Barcelona and Madrid.
- Andalusia estates, rural labor, agrarian inequality, and hunger politics.
- 1931, 1933, and 1936 election pressure shown through posters, newspapers, rallies, maps, and street scenes.

Likely future episode branches:

- The land question, especially Andalusia and rural labor.
- Republican-zone internal conflict: militias, state centralization, POUM/CNT/PCE tensions, Barcelona May Days.
- Nationalist coalition-building: military conspirators, Carlists, Falange, conservative Catholics, Army of Africa, and Franco consolidation.
- International witnesses and media: Orwell, Hemingway, Ivens, Capa, Taro, newsreels, propaganda, and foreign volunteers.
- Aftermath and memory: exile, repression, regional memory under Franco, transition-era silence, and later memory politics.
