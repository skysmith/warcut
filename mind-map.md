## Chronology

### Long Runway To War

- `1898-1923`
  - imperial decline, regime weakness, labor unrest, regional tensions
  - research use: what structural problems predate the republic?
- `1923-1930`
  - Primo de Rivera dictatorship, attempted stabilization from above
  - research use: what did the dictatorship suppress versus solve?
- `1931`
  - monarchy falls, Second Republic begins, reform expectations spike
  - active Warcut tie: the state-modernization problem in `the_contradiction`
- `1931-1933`
  - constitution, anticlerical conflict, military reform, agrarian reform, autonomy debates
  - active Warcut tie: the "who gets to define Spain?" line in `ep01`
- `1933-1935`
  - rightward turn, reform backlash, Asturias 1934, Catalan crisis
  - open need: make the center-feels-fragile story more specific before July 1936
- `Feb-Jul 1936`
  - Popular Front victory, polarization, strikes, assassinations, coup planning
  - active Warcut tie: `pressure_gauge`

### War Years

- `Jul-Nov 1936`
  - failed coup becomes fragmented civil war
  - Barcelona, Madrid, and many localities become contests over real authority
  - active Warcut tie: `cold_open_montage`, `coup_as_accelerant`, `mini_case_study_barcelona`
- `Late 1936-1937`
  - siege of Madrid, militia-to-army transition, Guernica, fall of the north, May Days in Barcelona
  - future branch: competing revolutionary versus state-centralizing projects inside the Republic
- `1938`
  - Teruel, Aragon collapse, Ebro, exhaustion
  - future branch: how momentum and logistics tilt decisively
- `1939`
  - Catalonia falls, Madrid collapses, mass exile, Francoist victory
  - future branch: endings, escapes, prisons, memory

### Aftermath Shadow

- Francoist repression and political exile
- regional memory under dictatorship
- transition-era silence versus later memory politics
- research use: this may become a separate memory/legacy episode track rather than part of `ep01`

## Factions

### Republican Camp

- republican state defenders
  - liberals, reformists, constitutional republicans
  - note target: how much real control do they retain after July 1936?
- socialist world
  - PSOE, UGT, Largo Caballero current, labor militancy, reform versus revolution tension
- anarchist world
  - CNT-FAI, strongest in Catalonia and Aragon, central for the "parallel power" story
  - active Warcut tie: Barcelona street authority and militia imagery
- anti-Stalinist revolutionary left
  - POUM and related factions
  - future branch: internal conflict inside the Republican zone
- communist current
  - PCE, PSUC, Soviet-linked centralization, discipline, military rebuilding
- regional autonomists within or alongside the Republic
  - Catalan institutions
  - Basque nationalists
  - key question: when is autonomy the main driver versus class or ideology?

### Nationalist / Rebel Camp

- military conspirators
  - Franco, Mola, Sanjurjo network, officer corps, Army of Africa
- Falangists
  - fascist style, mobilization, symbolism, youth energy
- Carlists and monarchists
  - especially strong in Navarre and conservative Catholic regions
- conservative Catholic and order-first bloc
  - landowners, church hierarchy, anti-revolutionary middle and upper sectors
- practical question for notes
  - avoid treating the Nationalist camp as ideologically simple; note coalition-building and later Francoist consolidation

### Civilian Social Blocs

- landless laborers, tenants, and rural poor
- industrial workers and union militants
- clergy and practicing Catholic communities
- regional language communities
- refugees, prisoners, and families under bombardment

  - Republican state continuity and later wartime governance

## Open Research Threads

- How do we explain "many Spains" without underplaying ideology, religion, and class?
  - next move: compare region-first framing against party-first framing with concrete local examples
- What is the cleanest `ep02` after `many spains`?
  - current best candidate: land question in Andalusia and Extremadura
- How should the Republican camp be shown after July 1936?
  - state, militias, unions, and committees coexist unevenly; this needs a clearer visual grammar
- Why do conservative Basques sometimes align with the Republic?
  - next target: autonomy promises, church position, and local threat perception
- What should represent the collapse of the center before the coup?
  - find the best mix of election maps, posters, newspapers, rally photos, and violence reporting
- Which early-war terror stories deserve separate treatment?
  - Badajoz, Seville repression, prison/execution patterns, anti-clerical violence, reprisals
- How much of the war should be told through regions versus fronts?
  - this affects whether Warcut branches by geography, theme, or strict chronology
- Where does empire belong in the story?
  - Morocco and the Army of Africa should probably move from side note to core causal thread
- What is the eventual shape of the project?
  - one long Spanish Civil War spine inside Warcut
  - or a broader Spanish history documentary map that later exceeds this repo
